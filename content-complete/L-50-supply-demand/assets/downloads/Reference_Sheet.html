Supply & Demand Reference

How market forces affect your personal finance decisions

LAW OF DEMAND

Definition: When price increases, quantity demanded decreases (and vice versa)

Personal Finance Example: When gas prices rise to $5/gallon, you drive less and carpool more. When they drop to $2.50/gallon, you take more road trips.

LAW OF SUPPLY

Definition: When price increases, quantity supplied increases (and vice versa)

Personal Finance Example: When salaries for nurses increase, more people enter nursing programs. When wages fall, fewer people pursue that career.

Demand Shifters
  • Income changes
  • Consumer preferences
  • Prices of related goods
  • Expectations about future
  • Number of buyers
Supply Shifters
  • Production costs
  • Technology improvements
  • Natural disasters
  • Government policies/taxes
  • Number of sellers
EQUILIBRIUM PRICE

Definition: The price where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied

Personal Finance Impact: Understanding equilibrium helps you predict when prices might be temporary vs. long-term. Example: A housing shortage pushes prices above equilibrium - expect higher rent until more apartments are built.

Personal Finance Applications
Housing: High demand + limited supply = rising rents. Time your apartment search during off-peak seasons.

Jobs: High demand for skills + low supply of workers = higher salaries. Choose careers with favorable supply/demand.

College Timing: Applying to colleges with lower applicant numbers (lower demand) can increase acceptance chances and financial aid.

Shopping: Buy seasonal items off-season when demand is low (winter coats in summer, ACs in winter).
💡 Key Takeaway: Supply and demand determine prices in free markets. Use this knowledge to time purchases, choose careers, and make smarter financial decisions.
