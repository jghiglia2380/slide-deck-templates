Real-World Examples

How Property Rights Affect Economic Development:
Hong Kong & Singapore:

Strong property rights protection has made these countries among the wealthiest in Asia. Entrepreneurs know their businesses and property are secure, encouraging massive investment.

United States:

Strong property rights and contract enforcement have been key to economic growth. Investors feel confident that their ownership will be protected by courts.

Venezuela:

Weak property rights and government seizures of businesses have led to economic collapse. Foreign investment dried up when companies feared losing their assets.

South Korea vs. North Korea:

Same people, same culture, different property rights systems. South Korea (strong rights) has GDP per capita 20x higher than North Korea (weak rights).
