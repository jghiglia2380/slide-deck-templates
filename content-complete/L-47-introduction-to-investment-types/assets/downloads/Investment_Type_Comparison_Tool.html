Stocks

High Risk
What it is: Ownership shares in a company
Historical Average Return: 10% annually (S&P 500)
Liquidity: Very High - can sell instantly during market hours
Minimum Investment: Price of 1 share (varies by stock)
Typical Fees: $0 (most brokers now fee-free)
Best For: Long-term growth, higher risk tolerance

Bonds

Low to Medium Risk
What it is: Loans to governments or corporations
Historical Average Return: 3-5% annually
Liquidity: Medium - can sell but may lose value
Minimum Investment: $1,000 (individual bonds), $25 (bond funds)
Typical Fees: 0-2% (bond funds)
Best For: Stable income, lower risk, diversification

Mutual Funds

Medium Risk
What it is: Professionally managed portfolio of stocks/bonds
Historical Average Return: 6-8% annually (varies by fund type)
Liquidity: High - can sell at end of trading day
Minimum Investment: $500-$3,000 (many have $0 minimum now)
Typical Fees: 0.5-2% expense ratio annually
Best For: Instant diversification, professional management

ETFs

Medium Risk
What it is: Baskets of stocks/bonds traded like stocks
Historical Average Return: 8-10% annually (index ETFs)
Liquidity: Very High - trade anytime during market hours
Minimum Investment: Price of 1 share ($50-$400 typically)
Typical Fees: 0.03-0.5% expense ratio annually
Best For: Low-cost diversification, flexibility
