Understanding the Spread

The bid-ask spread is the difference between the highest price a buyer is willing to pay (bid) and the lowest price a seller is willing to accept (ask). This spread represents a hidden transaction cost.

How to Calculate the Spread

Spread = Ask Price - Bid Price
Spread = $50.05 - $49.95 = $0.10
Spread % = (Spread ÷ Ask Price) × 100
Spread % = ($0.10 ÷ $50.05) × 100 = 0.20%

The Cost: The difference of $0.10 per share goes to market makers and represents your transaction cost. On 100 shares, you immediately lose $10 to the spread.

Key Insight: You lose money from the spread even if the stock price doesn't change. This is why frequent trading is expensive.

Spread Size Depends on Liquidity

Stock Type Example Spread Spread % Liquidity
Highly Liquid (e.g., AAPL, MSFT) $0.01 - $0.05 0.01% - 0.05% Very High
Moderately Liquid $0.10 - $0.25 0.20% - 0.50% Medium
Illiquid (Penny Stocks) $0.50+ 1% - 5%+ Low
Trading Tip: Always check the spread before trading. A spread wider than 0.5% is a warning sign. For small purchases, wide spreads can eat up 5-10% of your investment immediately.

Minimizing Spread Costs

Best Practices:

  • Trade liquid stocks: Popular stocks have tighter spreads (lower cost)
  • Use limit orders: Set your maximum buy price or minimum sell price
  • Avoid market orders on illiquid stocks: You might pay much more than expected
  • Trade during market hours: Pre-market and after-hours have wider spreads
  • Buy and hold: Frequent trading multiplies spread costs
