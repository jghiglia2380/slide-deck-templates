⏰ Stock Market Hours Reference Card

Quick reference for when U.S. stock markets are open and closed

Regular Trading Hours

9:30 AM - 4:00 PM Eastern Time
Monday through Friday (excluding holidays)
✓ Best time to trade: High liquidity, tight spreads, normal market conditions

Extended Trading Hours

Pre-Market: 4:00 AM - 9:30 AM ET
⚠️ Higher Risk: Lower volume, wider spreads, more volatility
Limited participants, prices may not reflect regular market values
After-Hours: 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM ET
⚠️ Higher Risk: Reduced liquidity, larger price swings possible
News and earnings reports often released during this time
Closed: Weekends & Holidays
No trading Saturday, Sunday, or on market holidays (see list below)
What is "Settlement" (T+2)?
When you buy or sell a stock, the transaction doesn't complete instantly. Settlement happens 2 business days after the trade date (T+2). This means:
• You won't receive sale proceeds for 2 days
• Stock ownership officially transfers 2 days after purchase
• This is important for avoiding "good faith violations" in cash accounts

📅 2025 Market Holidays (Markets Closed)

  • January 1 - New Year's Day
  • January 20 - Martin Luther King Jr. Day
  • February 17 - Presidents' Day
  • April 18 - Good Friday
  • May 26 - Memorial Day
  • June 19 - Juneteenth
  • July 4 - Independence Day
  • September 1 - Labor Day
  • November 27 - Thanksgiving Day
  • December 25 - Christmas Day

Note: If a holiday falls on a weekend, the market may be closed on the nearest weekday. Early closures (1:00 PM ET) typically occur on the day before Thanksgiving and Christmas Eve.

💡 When NOT to Trade

  • First 30 minutes (9:30-10:00 AM ET): Highest volatility as overnight orders execute and day traders enter. Prices can swing wildly.
  • Last 30 minutes (3:30-4:00 PM ET): "Power hour" with increased volume and volatility as traders close positions.
  • Pre-market/After-hours: Unless you're experienced and understand the risks. Spreads are wider and prices less reliable.
  • During major news: Federal Reserve announcements, earnings reports, etc. can cause extreme volatility.
  • On low-volume days: Day before holidays, summer Fridays - lower liquidity means higher spreads.

✓ Best Times to Trade

  • 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM ET: Most stable period with good liquidity and normal spreads
  • Mid-week (Tuesday-Thursday): Typically more stable than Mondays (news-heavy) or Fridays (position-closing)
  • Regular market hours only: Stick to 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET unless you have a specific reason to trade extended hours
⚠️ Important Reminders:
• Market orders placed outside regular hours may execute at very different prices
• Extended hours trading is not available at all brokerages
• Lower volume means your order might move the price more than expected
• For beginners: stick to regular trading hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET)
