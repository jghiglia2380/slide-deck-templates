Real-World Price Changes

How supply and demand dynamics affect prices in everyday situations

Understanding the Patterns
📊 Blue Line: Demand Curve
📊 Red Line: Supply Curve
↑ Price Increases: When supply decreases or demand increases
↓ Price Decreases: When supply increases or demand decreases
⛽🌪️
Hurricane → Gasoline Shortage
What Happened: A hurricane disrupts oil refineries, reducing gasoline production
Market Impact:
Supply decreases → Curve shifts left
Demand stays constant
Price ↑
🎯 When to buy: BEFORE the shortage if possible, or reduce usage and wait for recovery
⭐📱
Celebrity Endorsement → Product Popularity
What Happened: A popular celebrity endorses sneakers, making them highly desirable
Market Impact:
Demand increases → Curve shifts right
Supply stays constant (short-term)
Price ↑
🎯 When to buy: WAIT for the hype to die down or look for alternatives
📉💎
Economic Recession → Luxury Goods
What Happened: Economic downturn reduces consumer spending power
Market Impact:
Demand decreases → Curve shifts left
Supply stays constant
Price ↓
🎯 When to buy: NOW if you have stable income - sellers are motivated
🏭📦
New Factory → Increased Production
What Happened: A new manufacturing facility doubles production capacity
Market Impact:
Supply increases → Curve shifts right
Demand stays constant
Price ↓
🎯 When to buy: NOW - increased competition drives prices down
☀️❄️
Summer → Air Conditioning
What Happened: Heat wave hits, everyone wants to cool their homes
Market Impact:
Demand increases (seasonal) → Curve shifts right
Supply relatively fixed short-term
Price ↑
🎯 When to buy: FALL/WINTER when demand is low and sales are common
🌾🚜
Bumper Crop → Abundant Harvest
What Happened: Perfect weather produces record agricultural yields
Market Impact:
Supply increases → Curve shifts right
Demand stays constant
Price ↓
🎯 When to buy: NOW - stock up while prices are low (if non-perishable)
Key Takeaways for Smart Shopping
  • Track the Pattern: If you understand why prices change, you can predict when to buy
  • Seasonal Goods: Buy off-season when demand is low (AC in winter, coats in summer)
  • Supply Disruptions: Temporary shortages usually resolve - avoid panic buying at inflated prices
  • Demand Spikes: Hype-driven demand fades - patience saves money on trendy items
  • Economic Cycles: Recessions create buyer's markets for big purchases
