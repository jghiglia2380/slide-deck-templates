Understanding Supply and Demand:
The intersection of the supply and demand curves represents the equilibrium point, where the market naturally settles. At this point, the quantity suppliers want to sell exactly matches the quantity consumers want to buy at the equilibrium price.

Key Concepts

Equilibrium Price (P*): The price where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded ($50 in this example)
Equilibrium Quantity (Q*): The quantity traded at the equilibrium price (50 units in this example)
Market Forces: If price is above P*, surplus occurs; if below P*, shortage occurs
